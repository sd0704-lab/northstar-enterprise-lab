## 02 – Stakeholder Register

Northstar Precision Manufacturing (NPM) – Enterprise Cyber Defense Lab

## 1. Purpose
This document identifies all stakeholders associated with the Northstar Precision Manufacturing (NPM) Enterprise Cyber Defense Lab project. It defines their roles, influence, interest level, and engagement strategy to ensure proper communication, governance, and project alignment.

## 2. Stakeholder Identification Matrix

| Stakeholder                               | Role                            | Influence | Interest | Impact Level     | Engagement Strategy                                                  |
| ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- | --------- | -------- | ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Scott Dempsey                             | Project Lead / System Architect | High      | High     | Direct           | Full decision authority; responsible for execution and documentation |
| University of Phoenix Faculty             | Academic Oversight              | High      | Medium   | High             | Formal milestone submissions and periodic progress reporting         |
| Simulated Security Operations Team (SOC)  | Operational End Users           | Medium    | High     | Medium           | Environment designed to support detection workflows                  |
| Simulated IT Administration Team          | Infrastructure Support Role     | Medium    | Medium   | Medium           | Role-based access design and operational modeling                    |
| Future Incident Response Team (Simulated) | Security Operations Stakeholder | Medium    | High     | Medium           | Detection use-case validation and response simulation                |
| Virtualization Platform (Proxmox)         | Infrastructure Dependency       | Low       | Low      | High (Technical) | Maintain platform stability and version control                      |
| Open-Source Security Tools Community      | External Technical Resource     | Low       | Low      | Low              | Reference documentation and best practices alignment                 |

## 3. Stakeholder Classification

### High Influence / High Interest

* Scott Dempsey (Project Lead)

**Management Strategy:** Closely manage and directly control project direction.

### High Influence / Medium Interest

* University of Phoenix Faculty

**Management Strategy:** Keep satisfied through structured reporting and milestone compliance.

### Medium Influence / High Interest

* Simulated SOC Team
* Simulated Incident Response Team

**Management Strategy:** Keep informed; design environment to meet operational needs.

### Medium Influence / Medium Interest

* Simulated IT Administration Team

**Management Strategy:** Maintain alignment with identity and access control design.

### Low Influence / Low Interest

* Open-source tool maintainers

**Management Strategy:** Monitor documentation and updates as needed.

## 4. Stakeholder Communication Plan (High-Level)

| Stakeholder        | Communication Method                  | Frequency              |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Project Lead       | Direct oversight                      | Continuous             |
| University Faculty | Formal documentation submission       | Per academic milestone |
| Simulated SOC      | Documentation & configuration updates | Phase completion       |
| Simulated IT Team  | Design documentation review           | Phase completion       |

## 5. Stakeholder Authority & Governance

The Project Lead (Scott Dempsey) holds full authority for design decisions, risk mitigation, documentation standards, and implementation sequencing within the defined academic and technical constraints.

Academic faculty retain approval authority for grading and project validation.
